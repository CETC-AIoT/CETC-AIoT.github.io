Title of the workshop

IEEE iThings 2023 Workshop on Cloud-Edge-Terminal Collaboration and Aritificial Intelligence of Things: Building the Foundation of Industrial IoT Enabled Smart Manufacturing (CETC & AIoT 2023)

Objectives

Recently, with the new round of global technological revolution and the rapid progress of industry transformation, new opportunity has been occured for the digital, intelligent and green development of manufacturing system. The emergence of smart manufacturing will restructure various aspects of the manufacturing industry, including research and development, production, management, and services, significantly shorten and accurately optimize production cycles, and potientially enhance the adaptability of supply system.

Smart manufacturing has the characteristics of highly complex ecology and interactivity, which requires strict perofrmance on latency, quality of service (QoS), and efficiency of resource allocation. The Industrial IoT (IIoT) has been regarded as the critical way to solve the above problems. The smart manufacturing based on IIoT has two main challenges: 1) for multi-service-oriented and personalized manufacturing, the resource allocation of IIoT devices will become more complicated, requiring distributed, adaptive and artificial intelligence (AI)-based decision-making; 2) service requests and state parameters of IIoT system will be time-varying, which poses difficulties to the promptness of services and resource allocation. For the future development of smart manufacturing, these issues must be addressed.

Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) is emerged as an integrated technology that combines AI and IoT, which is expected to effectively help IIoT systems to mine valuable data reliably from the noisy and complex environment of smart manufacturing, paving the way for intelligent decision-making in multi-service-oriented demand and heterogeneous resource. Such AIoT architecture has encountered a great pressure on network communication and remote data processing, which hinder the deployment of time-critical applications. On this condition, cloud-edge-terminal collaboration is a promising technique to remedy these issues, which is a hot-spot technical way to achieve low latency, strong interaction, high scalability and fast service response for IIoT systems. Based on the cloud-edge and edge-terminal-based resource collaboration, the limited resources of IIoT devices can be fully utilized to provide users with high-quality instantaneous network service.

Scope

    The purpose of this workshop is to seek for practical solutions combining smart manufacturing with AIoT and cloud-edge-terminal architecture, and to discuss novel ideas, theories, frameworks, and testbeds for the promotion of future IIoT system. The topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • New architectures for AIoT-enabled IIoT
  • New architectures for Cloud-Edge-Terminal collaborative IIoT
  • Heterogeneous resource management for Cloud-Edge-Terminal collaborative AIoT
  • Multi-service-oriented resource management for AIoT-enabled IIoT
  • Intelligent computation and big data analytics for AIoT
  • Energy-efficient communication solutions for Cloud-Edge-Terminal collaborative AIoT
  • Security and privacy issues for AIoT-enabled IIoT
  • Proof-of-concept Cloud-Edge-Terminal collaborative and AIoT-enabled IIoT: experimental prototyping and testbeds
  • Cloud-Edge-Terminal collaborative smart manufacturing applications
  • Modeling, analysis and optimization of digital twin for AIoT-enabled IIoT
  • Architecutures, technologies, and applications of large neural network models for AIoT enabled IIoT

    Paper Submission

    Main conference papers are limited to 8 pages (regular paper), or 4 pages for poster papers following the IEEE Computer Society Proceedings Format, and are to be submitted as PDF via the site: https://cetc-aiot2023.edas.info/. Paper not following the IEEE templates will be rejected without reviews. Papers will be selected based on their originality, significance, relevance, and clarity of presentation assessed by at least three reviewers.

    Paper Publication

    Accepted conference papers will be published by IEEE (IEEE-DL and El indexed). At least one author of each accepted paper is required to register and present their work at the conference. Otherwise, the paper will not be included in the proceedings. Selected papers, after further extensions and revisions, will be recommended to special issues.
